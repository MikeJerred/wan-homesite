Mapping the relationship

My idea was to place the parents in the centre but separately, other parties involved will be placed around them at different distances depending upon how much they were affected by the event.

Connecting the dots

The relationships between the key parties such as the the parents and their friends and families are represented by a thicker red line, the thinner line represents their relationship with wider society.

Adding the element of shock

We wanted to add an element of shock to the model because we want the audience to experience how the parents felt when they discovered the death of the baby. We made a box that contains the model with a balloon hidden inside, the box represents the womb of the mother. We also added some red powder inside of the balloon so when it bursts it will paint the board.

Demonstrating the influence

After the balloon burst in the box, the red powder was left on the model representing how much each party was affected by the event. The more central the figure is the more he/she is affected as demonstrated in the model.
